# Coca-Cola-Sales-Analysis-Dashboard-using-Excel
This project presents a comprehensive Coca-Cola Sales Analysis Dashboard built entirely in Microsoft Excel. The purpose of this analysis is to transform raw transactional sales data into meaningful business insights that can support strategic decision-making in sales, retail partnerships, inventory planning, and product optimization.
The dashboard evaluates sales performance across:

Time periods and seasonality
Geographic regions
Retail partners
Beverage brands
Revenue and unit sales distribution

Using Excel’s analytical capabilities, the project uncovers operational trends, identifies high-performing markets, and highlights areas for strategic growth.

🎯 Business Problem

In the highly competitive beverage industry, companies must continuously optimize:

Product performance
Distribution efficiency
Retail relationships
Seasonal inventory planning
Revenue growth opportunities

This project answers the core business question:

How can Coca-Cola optimize its sales performance, distribution channels, and product mix across major U.S. retailers?

🧹 Data Preparation & Cleaning

Before analysis, the dataset underwent a complete preprocessing workflow to ensure data reliability and consistency.

Data Cleaning Steps Performed:
Standardized inconsistent column headers
Removed duplicate and unnecessary records
Handled missing and incomplete values
Reformatted transactional date fields into uniform date formats
Structured sales and unit metrics for aggregation
Verified retailer, region, and product naming consistency
Organized data into analysis-ready tables

These preparation steps ensured accurate Pivot Table calculations and smooth dashboard interaction.

📊 Dashboard Features

The dashboard includes multiple interactive visualizations to analyze sales performance from different perspectives.

1. Time-Based Sales Analysis
Monthly Sales Trend
Seasonal Revenue Peaks
Quarterly Demand Patterns
Key Insight:
Major revenue spikes occur in July and December, indicating strong seasonal demand during summer and holiday periods.

2. Regional Performance Analysis
Sales by Region
Geographic Market Share Comparison
Key Insight:
The West region contributes the highest share of revenue at 32.6%.
The Midwest shows comparatively lower performance and represents a growth opportunity.

3. Retailer Performance Analysis
Sales Contribution by Retail Partner
Retail Dependency Evaluation
Key Insight:
Over 50% of total revenue comes from a single retailer, Sodapop, highlighting both strong partnership value and concentration risk.

4. Brand Performance Analysis
Revenue by Beverage Brand
Units Sold by Brand
Volume vs Revenue Comparison
Key Insight:
Coca-Cola Classic leads in both sales revenue and units sold.
Sprite shows high unit movement but comparatively lower revenue contribution, suggesting possible pricing optimization opportunities.


📈 Key Business Insights

Seasonal Trends
Demand significantly increases during summer and holiday seasons.
Inventory and marketing campaigns should align with these periods.
Retail Dependency
Heavy dependence on one retailer creates operational risk.
Diversification across smaller retailers is recommended.
Regional Opportunities
Midwest market expansion can improve national balance and growth.
Product Pricing Strategy
Certain products have strong unit velocity but lower revenue efficiency.
Pricing and margin structures should be reassessed.


💡 Strategic Recommendations

1. Capitalize on Seasonal Demand
Increase promotional campaigns before July and December
Strengthen supply chain readiness during peak months

2. Reduce Retail Concentration Risk
Expand partnerships with underperforming retailers
Increase Midwest market penetration

3. Optimize Product Pricing
Reevaluate pricing strategy for high-volume products like Sprite
Improve profitability without sacrificing volume

4. Improve Retail Distribution
Focus on shelf placement and retailer-specific promotions
Use regional targeting to increase market share
